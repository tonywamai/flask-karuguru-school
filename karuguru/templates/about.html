History of this school

Karuguru Junior Academy School is a well-established learning institution in Kiambu County, Kenya. It has grown over the years to become one of the leading schools in the region that provides quality education to learners from different backgrounds.

Founding of the School

The school was established in the year 2006 by Rev. Samuel Ndungu. It started as a primary school with only one classroom (3 students and 2 teachers.)

Early Development

In its early years, the school faced many challenges, such as a lack of water, a lack of classrooms and toilets, and a lack of enough capital for development. Despite this, the commitment of the staff and support from the local community helped the school grow. The first head teacher was Lydia Mumbe, who was key in setting the foundation for discipline and academic excellence.

Growth and Achievements

Over the years, the school expanded its infrastructure by building more classes, dormitories, and laboratories. It has also introduced new programs such as computer classes, school clubs, and sports.

Current Status

Today, Karuguru Junior Academy has a population of over 300 students. It is managed by a Board of Management and the school continues to maintain high academic and co-curricular standards.
