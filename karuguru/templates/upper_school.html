Unlocking Potential in Our Upper School

In the upper school, we provide an inspiring environment where students in Grades 4 to 6 are empowered to grow into confident, capable learners. This critical stage bridges childhood and adolescence, and we are committed to nurturing academic excellence, personal responsibility, and leadership potential in every child. Our holistic approach ensures that students continue to feel supported, challenged, and motivated to reach their full potential.

Upper School Options

We welcome students into Grades 4, 5, and 6, offering a flexible and supportive environment tailored to meet the evolving needs of pre-adolescent learners. Our Upper School acts as a transition point between foundational learning in lower primary and the demands of secondary school, helping students develop the skills and confidence necessary for future academic success.

Our Approach to Learning

Learning in the Upper School is driven by curiosity, inquiry, and increasingly independent thought. Our teachers provide a balance of structured academic instruction and experiential learning opportunities, encouraging students to ask questions, explore topics in depth, and collaborate with their peers. We value critical thinking, creativity, and problem-solving, equipping students with essential skills for the future.

Facilities and Learning Environment

Students benefit from modern, well-equipped classrooms, inspiring outdoor play areas, libraries, sports fields, and chemical & ICT labs. Our safe and stimulating environment encourages exploration, discovery, and a healthy balance between academic and recreational activities.

Upper School Curriculum

The curriculum is broad and stimulating, offering a rich combination of core academic subjects and specialist disciplines. Students engage deeply with English, Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies, while also exploring ICT, Visual Arts, Performing Arts, and Physical Education. We place strong emphasis on literacy, numeracy, research skills, and project-based learning, fostering well-rounded intellectual development.

School Community and Parent Involvement

We believe that education is a partnership between school and home. Our strong sense of community is supported through regular parent-teacher communication, school events, and opportunities for parental involvement, creating a supportive network for every child's development.
